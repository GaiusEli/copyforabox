# AgencyBox App Flow Document

## Onboarding and Sign-In/Sign-Up

When a new user visits AgencyBox, they are greeted by a warm, informative landing page that explains the core value of the platform – an AI-driven system that makes setting up Meta ad campaigns quick and easy. Here, the user sees clear call-to-action buttons inviting them to sign up or log in. For those who are new, the sign-up flow offers several methods: a traditional email registration flow, as well as social logins through providers supported by Clerk.dev, including SSO for enterprise users. During sign-up, basic information is collected and the user is presented with a simple interface that guides them through verifying their email and setting up their password. Additionally, if an existing user forgets their password, there is an easy-to-find “Forgot Password” link that triggers a secure password recovery process. Once signed in, users are automatically directed to a page where they can connect their Meta Ads account using Facebook/Instagram OAuth. This secure connection is an essential first step and is presented in a clear, step-by-step modal that explains why connecting the Meta account is necessary and what permissions are required. Signing out is straightforward, with a persistent profile menu making it simple to switch accounts or log out, ensuring that even non-technical users can manage their sessions without hassle.

## Main Dashboard or Home Page

After a successful sign-in and Meta account connection, the user lands on the main dashboard – the central hub for all app activities. The dashboard immediately presents a clean and modern layout featuring a sidebar navigation with options for Campaigns, Performance, Settings, and additional help from the AI assistant. At the top, a header displays essential information such as the current workspace name and user profile with role details. Widgets on the dashboard provide quick insights into recent campaign performance, upcoming scheduled campaigns, and usage statistics like remaining AI generation credits. The design uses a blue-purple gradient for primary buttons and accent elements, consistent with the AgencyBox brand, and the page is structured so that users can easily click through to start a new campaign, review existing campaigns, or update account settings. Navigation between sections is seamless, as clicking on any menu item either opens a dedicated page or loads content dynamically within the dashboard. The overall design emphasizes clarity and ease of use so that both solo entrepreneurs and team-based accounts quickly grasp where to find the features they need.

## Detailed Feature Flows and Page Transitions

The heart of AgencyBox lies in its step-by-step campaign creation wizard, which begins immediately after the user chooses to create a new campaign from the dashboard. In the first step of the wizard, the user is required to connect their Meta Ads account if it has not been connected already, ensuring that the permission flow via OAuth is complete. Once the account is linked, the wizard presents an intuitive form where the user defines the campaign objective from options like Awareness, Traffic, or Conversions. At this stage, they also set a campaign budget and schedule, with input fields validated to meet Meta’s guidelines. The design gently guides users through choosing their target audience by offering preset selections with demographic fields such as location and age, sometimes enhanced with contextual tips to drive the best results. Each step includes clear instructions and a forward/back navigation, allowing the user to review and modify previous entries without losing any settings.

After configuring the campaign basics, the journey moves into creative asset management. The interface offers two paths for adding creatives: the user can either upload images or short videos directly from their device or choose to generate new media using the integrated AI service, Sora. If the user opts to generate assets, a modal appears where they can enter a descriptive text prompt. The platform then processes the AI call and displays the resulting creative asset, along with options to refine the prompt. Once three creative assets are available – in line with the 3:2:2 methodology – the wizard transitions to the ad copy generation phase. Here, the user inputs a brief product or service description and selects a tone. GPT-4 is then called to produce two variants of primary text and two creative headline options. The interface displays two text boxes for each category complete with live character counters. Users can accept the AI suggestions or manually edit the copies. This stage is crafted so that even non-experts feel guided and supported.

Following the generation of creative assets and text, the wizard presents a review screen where all 12 ad variations (formed by combinations of three creatives, two ad texts, and two headlines) are laid out in a grid or carousel format. Along with a visual preview, a summary panel recaps the campaign’s objective, budget, schedule, and targeting criteria. For enterprise users with specific role restrictions, a “Submit for Approval” button replaces the direct publish option, ensuring that any Editor’s modifications must be endorsed by an Admin before the campaign is launched. Once the user confirms the settings in the review stage, clicking the final submission button sends a series of secure serverless function calls that interact with Meta’s Marketing API. These calls handle the creation of the campaign, ad set, and ad creative, ensuring that Campaign Budget Optimization and dynamic creative methods are properly set up. On successful submission, the user sees a clear confirmation message and is redirected to the performance analytics page.

After campaign submission, the navigation flow shifts to the performance dashboard where users can monitor the status of their active campaigns. Detailed view pages include time-series charts for metrics like impressions, clicks, CTR, CPA, and ROAS. The dashboard also features an AI-driven recommendations area that provides strategic advice based on campaign performance. Throughout this flow, transitions between pages are designed to be smooth and intuitive, ensuring that users always know how to move from one step to the next in their campaign lifecycle.

## Settings and Account Management

The Settings and Account Management section is designed so that users can easily update their personal information, modify workspace preferences, and change notification settings. Accessible via a clear menu option in the dashboard’s sidebar, this section allows users to manage their profile details – including changing passwords and updating contact data – and configure advanced settings such as linking or switching connected Meta accounts and adjusting AI generation quotas if necessary. For enterprise and mid-market users, there are additional controls to manage team collaboration; Admins can invite or remove team members, alter user roles, and access an audit log that tracks critical changes. When billing is integrated through Stripe for subscription management, this area will also include options to view current plan details, upgrade or downgrade the subscription, and review past invoices. After finishing any changes, the user can easily navigate back to the dashboard by clicking a persistent “Back to App” button, ensuring that account management feels like a natural extension of the main user experience rather than a separate silo.

## Error States and Alternate Paths

AgencyBox is built with robust error handling to ensure that users always know what to do if something goes wrong. If a user enters invalid data, the system displays clear inline error messages that explain the specific issue and how to correct it. For example, if a budget amount falls below Meta’s minimum requirement, the input is highlighted with a concise error message. In cases of connectivity issues, such as a temporary loss of internet or Meta API timeouts, the app displays a friendly notification indicating that the system is having trouble connecting and advises the user to check their connection or try again later. If a user attempts an action that is not allowed based on their role – for instance, an Editor trying to publish a campaign without admin approval – the interface disables the button and shows a tooltip explaining the restriction. When serverless functions encounter unexpected errors or if an external API does not respond, a dedicated error page is shown with an option to retry the action, and audit logs are automatically updated to help technical support diagnose the problem. These error states are designed to be informative and non-disruptive so that users remain confident in their overall journey even when encountering issues.

## Conclusion and Overall App Journey

From the moment a new user lands on AgencyBox through the complete campaign lifecycle, the platform is crafted to be transparent, supportive, and intelligent. Starting with a simple sign-up process that offers multiple authentication methods and secure password recovery, users are guided seamlessly into the heart of the application where they connect their Meta Ads account. The campaign creation wizard leads them through a clear, step-by-step workflow: from choosing campaign objectives, setting budgets and schedules, and defining target audiences to adding creative assets and generating ad copy. At every stage, contextual help, clear instructions, and the power of AI work in tandem to simplify decisions and produce high-quality ads. Once the campaign is reviewed and submitted, the user is directed to a performance dashboard that not only displays key metrics in an accessible manner but also provides AI-driven recommendations to improve results. Settings and account management pages offer comprehensive control over personal and team data, while thoughtful error handling ensures that any issues are communicated clearly, allowing users to remain on track. In summary, AgencyBox delivers a seamless, end-to-end experience that empowers everyone from solo entrepreneurs to large enterprise teams to manage their Meta ad campaigns effortlessly and confidently.
